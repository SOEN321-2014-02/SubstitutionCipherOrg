i remember him looking round the cover and whistling to himself as he did so and then breaking out in that old sea song that he sang so often afterwards in the high old tottering voice that seemed to have been tuned and broken at the capstan bars then he rapped on the door with a bit of stick like a handspike that he carried and when my father appeared called roughly for a glass of rum this when it was brought to him he drank slowly like a connoisseur lingering on the taste and still looking about him at the cliffs and up at our signboard
